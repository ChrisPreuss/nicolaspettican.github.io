about me


I come from a small sea-side town on the East Coast of Spain. Here I volunteered in different organisations (ADAHMA, C.A.B.) and even learned to be a small scale entrepreneur by selling goods grown in my orchard. I did my A-levels in a Spanish college, where I had to be examined on 10 different subjects including maths, chemistry, physics, biology, English language, English grammar, philosophy, Spanish literature, Catalan language, and history of Spain. Having done the non-science subjects proved very useful in acquiring essay writing skills that helped me along my career. After finishing college I came to study at the University of Manchester in 2012 to pursue my ambition of learning more about biological sciences as well as business and management.


During my undergraduate course, BSc Biotechnology with Enterprise, I learned a great deal about biological sciences, pharmacology and bioethics while also learning about market research, management, entrepreneurship and other business studies. I gained experience in the lab by culturing cells and bacteria, performing genetic modifications, PCR and protein purification among other techniques. At the same time I gained experience in market research, leadership and teamwork through coursework set by the Manchester Business School. My extracurricular activities included running the Faculty of Life Sciences’ Society for which I was elected 1st year representative in 1st year, marketing and events manager in 2nd year and vice president in my 3rd and final year. I participated in solidarity and cultural events through other societies such as NHSF, practiced sports such as athletics and weightlifting and was even mentored by life coaches, which increased my self-motivation and interpersonal skills. During my time in halls of residence I was democratically elected event manager, working towards providing a fun and enjoyable year for the residents. To fund my studies I had to work part-time throughout my degree in public relations, event management and even sales management in different local companies. This allowed me to acquire skills in communication, sales techniques, marketing strategies and management.


In the summer before starting my final year I undertook a research project at the University of Sheffield with Dr Hughes funded by the BSPP in which I looked at the differences in the metabolome during fungal infection in wheat. While doing my project I was privileged to accompany Dr Hughes across the UK to aid in his research and attend the BSPP Presidential Conference.


During my final year I attended the XING business game, aimed at designing and presenting a business plan together with your team, for which I got awarded for best presenter. I volunteered for different events organised by bodies in the University of Manchester such as Outreach Events and Multilingual Manchester. A highlight of my final year is a project set by the MBS, where I had to develop a product and deliver a pitch in order to secure funding, which taught me valuable skills for business. My final year project, adjacent to the former, involved the use of computational work in order to study the variability of a particular class of P450 enzymes: P450-BM3. This led me to realise the need to improve my computational skills, which led to my next stepping stone in life: to undertake a Master's in Computational Biology. I then graduated with a 2.1 with honours in the summer of 2015.


Before starting my Master’s I needed to fund it, thus I acquired a catering job in Warner Brother’s Studio London. Despite not being part of my field of study or expertise, this job proved to be a challenge that I managed to surpass. The constant pressure of serving on average 5000 customers a day improved my endurance and the reward scheme for attaining the highest sales pushed me to eventually achieve the highest number of average “spend per head” in my department. During my spare time I learned about coding and optimised my skills in IT.


At the present time I am completing a Master’s in Computational Biology (Bioinformatics), considered one of the fastest growing field of biology which may have the biggest impact on the health industry in the near future. Despite the steep learning curve I have now become more computer savvy. I have learned to program in different languages such as Python 2.7, Perl, R and Java and during my spare time I take online courses to learn more about Python and web designing with HTML, CSS and JS. I have also learned a great deal about data analysis, acquiring skills which I currently apply daily for my projects. As another highlight I co-founded the Bioinformatics Society, which delivers workshops and talks about techniques and resources that can aid researchers and students when analysing their data. I am also currently working for the University as a Student Ambassador, providing tours, information and answering queries through emails and telephone calls to prospective undergraduate and postgraduate students.


I am quite proactive, so my day to day activities outside of my degree and work include taking online courses, exercising outside or in the gym, creating Animations and written content for my future blog and planning ahead for innovative projects and trips. I enjoy science communication and I try to learn as much of a topic as I can before giving my view and opinion. In fact I am in the process of creating a blog where I can share my views and communicate science in a simple and understandable way as well as share lifestyle advice which I apply for self-motivation and improving my focus and drive.
