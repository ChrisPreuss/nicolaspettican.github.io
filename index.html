Below are the highlighted achievements and roles

2016 - Present

Postgraduate Student Ambassador
University of Manchester

Currently provide tours, activities and general information for prospective undergraduates and postgraduate students (home and international) in the Faculty of Life Sciences while answering their queries about the university, culture and student life over emails and social media and through the Manchester Calling Program.

2015 - Present

Committee executive
Bioinformatics Society

The current president and co-founder of the Bioinformatics Society. Represents the society as figurehead, secures funding and sets goals and objectives whilst overseeing their fulfilment. The society delivers workshops and talks about techniques and resources that can aid researchers and students when analysing their biological data.

2015 - Present

MSc in Bioinformatics and System Biology
University of Manchester

Currently completing a Master's degree in order to improve on computational skills and to learn about the newest fields of biology such as genomics, proteomics and systems biology. Extracurricular life involves learning to code different languages, design websites and participate in charity events. Also keeps active by regularly exercising and keeping a well-balanced diet.

2012 - 2015

BSc in Biotechnology with Enterprise
University of Manchester

This degree included a wide range of modules from different fields of biology such as microbiology, molecular biology, biochemistry, pharmacology, neuroscience, endocrinology and the 'omics. It was also linked with Manchester Business School, designed to teach how to conduct market research, create business plans, secure funding from venture capitalists and to learn the basics about management. Graduated in the summer of 2015 with a 2.1 with honours.

2012 - 2015

Committee executive
Faculty of Life Science's Society

Elected to run the Faculty of Life Sciences Society for three years in the following positions: 1st year representative, marketing and events manager and vice president. During the time in the committee, FOLSS managed to pay off the 2k debt the society was in (created in the previous management) and increased the society's budget to surpass 4 figures. Additionally, designed the society's logo and marketing material such as posters and flyers.

2014

Undergraduate researcher
University of Sheffield

Carried out a lab-based, BSPP funded, plant pathology project. Analised the differences in the metabolome during fungal infection in wheat. Developed new laboratory skills in microscopy and MS and improved time management skills. Attended the BSPP presidential conference in St Andrews.
